Arts & Culture in Kigali

Rwanda's culture is woven from tradition, resilience, art, music, dance — and a spirit of community unlike anywhere else on earth.

Home / Culture
Rich Heritage

A Culture of Resilience
and Creativity

Rwanda's culture is extraordinarily rich — rooted in centuries of Kinyarwanda traditions, shaped by colonial history, and reborn through remarkable national resilience. Kigali today is a city pulsing with creative energy, where contemporary art meets ancient tradition at every turn.

From the vivid contemporary paintings of Inema Arts Center to the rhythmic thunder of Intore dancers, from the colorful chaos of Kimironko Market to the haunting songs of Rwanda's iconic inanga harp — Kigali's culture is alive, evolving, and endlessly fascinating.

  • Intore Dance — Rwanda's sacred warrior dance tradition
  • Flourishing contemporary art galleries
  • Agaseke basket weaving — a UNESCO-recognized craft

The top cultural experiences every visitor to Kigali should seek out

Visual Art

Inema Arts Center

Kacyiru, Kigali

East Africa's most celebrated contemporary art gallery was founded by brothers Emmanuel and Innocent Nkurunziza. The space features rotating exhibitions of vibrant paintings, sculptures, and mixed-media works — predominantly by Rwandan artists. Weekend dance performances and open studio sessions make it a community hub, not just a gallery.

Gallery + Studio Weekend performances ️ Art for sale

Market

Kimironko Market

Kimironko, Kigali

Kigali's largest and most energetic market is an overwhelming sensory experience — bolts of Ankara fabric in every color, fresh tropical produce, handmade baskets, second-hand clothing, electronics, and the constant hum of bargaining in Kinyarwanda. Arrive early for the freshest produce and least crowds. Bargaining is expected and respected.

Open: 6am–6pm daily Crafts & Fabrics Best prices in Kigali

Heritage

Rwanda Cultural Village

Kandt House, Kigali

Step into traditional Rwandan life at the Cultural Village, a living museum that showcases authentic inzu (traditional round houses), traditional dress, food preparation, and cultural demonstrations. Knowledgeable guides explain the significance of each tradition in the context of Rwandan society, both past and present. Perfect for families and history enthusiasts.

️ Living museum ‍‍ Family friendly Photography allowed

Museum

Kandt House Museum

Nyarugenge, Kigali

Kandt House is the oldest building in Kigali, built by German explorer Richard Kandt in 1907 — the man credited with founding Kigali as Rwanda's capital. Today it serves as the Natural History Museum of Rwanda, featuring exhibitions on Rwanda's ecology, history, and the fascinating story of how this small, landlocked nation became what it is today.

️ Natural History Museum Mon–Sat: 9am–4pm Small entrance fee

Music

Nyundo Music School

Western Province (Day Trip)

Just two hours from Kigali, the Nyundo School of Music is Rwanda's most prestigious music conservatory, teaching traditional instruments like the inanga (zither), ingoma (drums), and iningiri (fiddle). Visit for a live student performance or attend one of the cultural showcases held throughout the year. A truly moving experience.

Live performances 2hr from Kigali By appointment

Craft

Agaseke Basket Weaving

Women's Cooperatives, Kigali

Rwanda's iconic Agaseke baskets are woven from natural fibers using centuries-old techniques passed down through generations of women. Each basket tells a story through its geometric patterns and colors. Visit one of Kigali's women's weaving cooperatives to watch artisans at work and purchase authentic pieces that directly support local communities.

UNESCO recognized craft Women-led cooperatives ️ Available to purchase

Top 5 Cultural Etiquette Rules

Respect these customs to connect authentically with locals

  1. Greet before asking — Always exchange greetings before any conversation or request
  2. Use both hands — When giving or receiving items, use both hands as a sign of respect
  3. Dress modestly — Cover shoulders and knees, especially in villages and memorials
  4. Ask before photographing — Always seek permission before taking someone's photo
  5. Remove shoes — Take off footwear before entering traditional homes or sacred spaces
