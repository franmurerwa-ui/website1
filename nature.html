Rwanda's Natural Wonders

A Country Reborn
in Green

Rwanda's extraordinary commitment to conservation has made it one of Africa's greatest nature travel destinations. The country is home to nearly half the world's remaining mountain gorillas, three distinct ecosystems, and an explosion of biodiversity that continues to recover and thrive after decades of protection.

From Kigali, you are less than 3 hours from gorilla trekking, safari game drives, chimpanzee tracking, and some of the most dramatic highland landscapes on the continent. Nature in Rwanda is not just a backdrop — it is the story.

  • ~1,000 mountain gorillas — half the world's population
  • Big Five wildlife in Akagera National Park
  • Golden monkeys unique to the Virunga region
  • Nyungwe: Africa's oldest rainforest
  • 700+ bird species recorded across Rwanda

Volcanoes National Park

Musanze, Northern Province

The crown jewel of Rwanda's national parks, Volcanoes National Park is home to five of the eight dormant Virunga Volcanoes and serves as one of the only sanctuaries on earth where you can trek on foot to visit mountain gorillas in their natural habitat. The experience is life-changing — sitting within meters of a silverback gorilla family is humbling beyond words.

The park also harbors golden monkeys, forest buffalo, and over 200 bird species. Hikers can summit Mount Bisoke for crater lake views or explore the Dian Fossey research site.

Akagera National Park

Eastern Province

Rwanda's only savanna park is home to elephants, lions, leopards, hippos, giraffes, zebras, and Africa's largest herd of topi antelope. Reintroduced black rhinos now roam the park's open grasslands alongside lions returned from South Africa. Game drives, boat safaris on Lake Ihema, and guided walks make Akagera a complete East African safari experience.

Nyungwe Forest National Park

Western Province

One of Africa's oldest and most biodiverse rainforests, Nyungwe is a primordial world of giant ferns, cathedral trees, and mist. Home to 13 primate species including chimpanzees and colobus monkeys, the park offers chimp tracking, the thrilling canopy walkway suspended 70m above the forest floor, and hundreds of bird species.

Gishwati-Mukura National Park

Western Province

Rwanda's newest national park, established in 2015, is a conservation success story. Once severely deforested, Gishwati-Mukura has been painstakingly restored and now harbors habituated chimpanzees, golden monkeys, and over 230 bird species. Community-run tourism ensures local families benefit directly from every visit.

Top 6 Things to Pack

Essential gear for Rwanda wildlife experiences

  1. Waterproof hiking boots — Trails are muddy; ankle support is essential
  2. Insect repellent (DEET 50%+) — Especially for rainforest treks
  3. Layered clothing — Mountains are cold; forests are humid
  4. Binoculars — Essential for birdwatching and spotting wildlife
  5. Rain jacket — Rwanda's tropical climate means sudden showers
  6. Camera with extra batteries — Wildlife moments won't wait

Wildlife Etiquette

Respect the animals and the environment

  • Keep 7 meters distance from gorillas at all times
  • Wear a mask near gorillas — human diseases can kill them
  • No flash photography around wildlife
  • Keep noise to a minimum on all treks
  • ️ Carry out all rubbish — leave no trace
  • Stay on designated trails only
  • No smoking in national parks
  • Never feed wildlife — it disrupts their behavior
