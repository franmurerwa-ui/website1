From sizzling brochettes to fine dining with city views — Kigali's food scene is booming and unforgettable.

Kigali Food Scene

A Culinary Journey Through
Rwanda's Capital

Kigali's restaurant landscape has exploded in recent years, transforming the city into a legitimate foodie destination. You'll find everything from authentic Rwandan street food stalls to glamorous rooftop restaurants serving international cuisine.

Local staples include isombe (cassava leaves with peanut sauce), ibihaza (pumpkin stew), brochettes (grilled meat skewers), and ugali (maize porridge). Fresh Lake Victoria tilapia is a must-try.

  • Brochettes — Kigali's beloved street food
  • Fresh Nile Perch & Tilapia from Lake Kivu
  • Organic, farm-to-table restaurants
  • Wine bars and cocktail lounges
  • Rwanda's world-famous specialty coffee

Heaven Restaurant & Bar

Kiyovu, Kigali

International / Fine Dining

Perched on a hill with sweeping views of Kigali, Heaven is consistently rated one of East Africa's best restaurants. Their menu blends local Rwandan ingredients with international techniques. The rooftop terrace is perfect for sundowners while watching Kigali's skyline glow at dusk.

7am–10pm daily Reservations recommended

Repub Lounge

Kacyiru, Kigali

½ 4.6
Bar & Grill / Live Music

Repub Lounge is the city's most beloved entertainment hub, combining great food with live music almost every night. Known for their brochettes, burgers, and cocktails, it attracts both locals and expats. The lively atmosphere and outdoor seating make it a social landmark in Kigali.

Poivre Noir

Remera, Kigali

4.9
French / European Fine Dining

Poivre Noir is Kigali's premier fine dining establishment, run by award-winning Belgian chef Filip Verheyden. The intimate restaurant offers a sophisticated European menu with Rwandan accents. Their tasting menu is a culinary masterpiece, featuring locally sourced ingredients elevated by classical French technique.

Tue–Sun: 6pm–10pm Reservations essential

Nyamirambo Street Food

Nyamirambo, Kigali

½ 4.7
Rwandan Street Food

No visit to Kigali is complete without eating at the street food stalls of Nyamirambo. Charcoal-grilled brochettes (goat, beef, or chicken on skewers) are served with plantain, chips, or isombe. Pair it with an ice-cold Primus beer and you have the quintessential Kigali experience — for just a few dollars.

Evenings from 5pm From 1,000 RWF

Top 6 Rwandan Dishes to Try

  1. Brochettes — Charcoal-grilled meat skewers, Rwanda's ultimate street food
  2. Isombe — Cassava leaves cooked in peanut sauce, hearty and earthy
  3. Ibihaza — Pumpkin stew, a comforting traditional staple
  4. Ugali — Thick maize porridge, served alongside stews and vegetables
  5. Tilapia wa kutungika — Whole fried Nile tilapia from Lake Kivu
  6. Mandazi — Soft East African doughnuts, perfect for breakfast

Rwanda's Drink Culture

  • Rwandan Coffee — Specialty single-origin coffee, world-class quality
  • Rwandan Tea — Sweet, milky chai from the highland tea estates
  • Primus Beer — Rwanda's most popular lager, refreshing and crisp
  • Mutzig — Premium Rwandan lager from Gisenyi
  • Urwagwa — Traditional fermented banana wine
  • Fresh Fruit Juice — Papaya, mango, and passion fruit blends
  • Herbal Teas — Made from local lemongrass and mint
  • Water — Always drink bottled or filtered water in Kigali
